# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Individuals and households tracking day-to-day money on their phone first. They open the app quickly to log a purchase, check what's left, pay a card, or glance at the month. They are not finance professionals — they want clarity and calm, not spreadsheets. Often used both in daylight (shopping) and at night (reconciling), so light and dark must both be first-class.

## Product Purpose
MyMoney is a local-first PWA for tracking accounts, spending, transfers, budgets, recurring items, goals, and now credit cards — with optional Firebase backup/household sync. Data lives in the browser first; nothing requires an account. Success looks like a person trusting the numbers at a glance and logging activity in seconds without friction.

## Brand Personality
Warm, calm, trustworthy. Money is stressful for most people; the interface should feel like a supportive, well-made personal ledger — approachable and human, never cold-corporate-bank or hype-fintech. Three words: warm, clear, grounded.

## Anti-references
- Generic blue fintech-SaaS dashboards (the old look): identical white-card grids, the hero-metric template, flat gray-on-white, navy primary. This is exactly what we are moving away from.
- Cold navy/black "trading terminal" dark mode.
- Crypto/hype aesthetics (the old ₿ logo), gradients-as-decoration, glassmorphism.

## Design Principles
- **Numbers are the hero.** Tabular figures, clear positive/negative language, a balance you can read in a glance. Color and size serve legibility, not decoration.
- **Warmth in the accent, not the page.** A warm terracotta/coral accent and warm ink carry the personality; surfaces stay clean and high-contrast for dense financial data (no cream/sand body).
- **Calm over busy.** Real hierarchy and breathing room instead of repeated equal-weight cards. One primary thing per screen.
- **The tool disappears.** Familiar, consistent affordances; every control has its full state set. Mobile-first, fast, offline.
- **Honest money.** Assets and debt are shown plainly; credit utilization and what-you-owe are never hidden behind happy framing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, in both themes. Never rely on color alone for positive/negative — pair with sign and label.
- Full keyboard support and visible focus rings on all interactive controls.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`; motion is feedback, not spectacle.
- Light and dark both maintained to AA; warm dark mode, not cold navy.
